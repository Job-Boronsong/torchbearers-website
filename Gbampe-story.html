A Story of Hope and Healing from Gbampe

Her ordeal began with what seemed like a simple fall from a stool while carrying out her household duties. What followed, however, was eight long months of being bedridden.

When we met her, she was unable to sit up on her own, let alone walk. Since then, we have visited her periodically—praying with her, offering encouragement, and providing material support as the Lord enables us.

By the grace of God and through our continued prayers, her condition has improved significantly. Today, she is able to walk again—something that once seemed impossible. However, she does so with the aid of a walking stick due to stiffness in her knee joint. One of our team members, a nurse, explained that because she had been lying down for so long without movement, the joint has become stiff and needs medical attention.

While we recognize that the situation now requires physical treatment—possibly involving hospital care, therapy, or traditional bone-setting—the challenge remains financial. Her health insurance has expired, and we are uncertain about the costs involved in seeking the medical help she needs. As a newly established ministry with limited resources, we are cautious not to start what we may not be able to sustain.

Despite these hurdles, discipleship is ongoing. Our nurse continues to engage her spiritually, teaching and encouraging her in her walk with the Lord even as we trust God for the next steps in her physical healing.

In the photo, Mary Abdulai, the team leader, is seen standing beside the old woman with a child in her arms—a symbol of compassion, leadership, and the generational hope we strive to spread. This is more than just a story of physical recovery—it is a testimony of faith, compassion, and the transforming power of love in action.
